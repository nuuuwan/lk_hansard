Before I proceed further with my limited address on the identified areas, I wish to highlight to this august Assembly two glaring deficiencies in the Report. Based on views expressed by market participants, non-participation of the State banks was the biggest contributor in the execution of this outright scam through the two tainted auctions. Market participants are strongly of the view that the State banks, if they had participated, would have scooped up a big majority of the Bonds offered in the two tainted issues at much lower yields, close to market yields, thus preventing or significantly reducing the loss suffered by the Government through these two issues. To date, there is no justifiable basis or answers to support the non-participation of the State banks.

Madam Presiding Member, the second point is the loss caused to the general public because of the sharp spike in yield rates after the two tainted auctions. Several attempts have been made to compute the loss suffered by the Government, but no attempt has been made to date to compute the secondary market loss suffered by the general public who were holding bonds and who were forced to sell due to the artificial spike in the market yield, which resulted in margin calls by banks on their leveraged positions. The question that needs answers in

relation to this is, which primary dealer scooped up all these Bonds from the secondary market and were those also sold to the EPF later on when rates stabilized? Madam Presiding Member, I am of the firm view that the general public deserves answers to these two questions.

Coming back to the subject of my address, what is glaringly evident from the Reports is the callous manner the individuals politically exposed to the Government of the day, whether it be the previous Government, the last Government or the present rulers, have blatantly gone about manipulating the orderly working of the Central Bank by influencing the highest officers of the Central Bank to enrich a selected group of individuals at a great cost to the general public.

Every officer working for or holding post in the institutions identified, namely the Central Bank, the Governor, the Monetary Board and the Employees Provident Fund, is a fiduciary. A fiduciary is legally defined as a relationship in trust, especially in relation to the trustee and the beneficiaries. In the said instant, the institutions identified are trustees of the citizens, who are the beneficiaries. The duty of a fiduciary entails the highest standard of care in equity or law towards the beneficiaries, to whom the duty is owed. The highest standard of care requires a fiduciary to act prudently with due skill, care, diligence and judgement in relation to the affairs of the beneficiaries. Madam Presiding Member, the persons identified in the Forensic Audit Reports related to the institutions that I have highlighted are all professionals entrusted with trust under a fiduciary relationship, who owe a fiduciary duty to the general public, purportedly with competence to administer and manage monies belonging to the people of this country; in this instant, the Central Banks monies and the beneficiaries of the Employees Provident Fund.

This House should also take due note of the fact that in relation to administration and management of public monies, globally the bar set for prudence in relation to fiduciary duties is at a very high level. Coming back to the conclusions in the Reports, this august Assembly should take due note that, leave alone the prudent rule, even basic rules of prudence through average skill, care, diligence and judgement have been alien not only to the individuals identified in the Report, but also to those at the Central Bank, the institution entrusted with statutory responsibility to maintain economic and price stability and financial stability as an institution that enforces rules of prudence in the financial sector in the country and the Employees Provident Fund, a statutory body set up to manage a social security scheme, which is managed by the Central Bank.
