Those are also with regard to the jurisdiction of the courts. Sir, as you are aware, the Northern and the Eastern Provinces were merged at a particular time. During the period when both the provinces were merged, they were functioning together and certain statutes had been passed by the North Eastern Provincial Council. But now, there is a practical issue because they made those statues as the Provincial Council of the merged North-East Provinces and there are certain financial statutes that had been adopted by them. But, later on, the Supreme Court made a Determination, I think the Hon. Anura Dissanayake knows, when the JVP went to the Supreme Court that the merger was illegal and accordingly, the two provinces were demerged. As a result of the demerger of the two provinces, now the question arises as to how to recover the defaulted taxes under the existing financial statutes, because at the time when the statutes were passed, it was one merged area and had one provincial legal system. So, now, after the demerger of the two provinces, there is a practical issue as to which court has the power to recover the defaulted taxes and levies. For that purpose, we will have to give jurisdictions separately. That is, for the courts sitting in the Northern Province to collect all defaulted revenues in that province and similarly, if there are taxes and levies defaulted by people who are obliged to pay in the Eastern Province, we are empowering the courts in the Eastern Province to collect them. It is only for that purpose we have made these two Regulations under the Judicature Act, which I have presented today.

Presiding Member, the current economic situation in Sri Lanka is challenging, characterized by low GDP growth, high public debt, fiscal deficits, balance of payments issues, a depreciating currency and rising inflation. Sri Lanka's economy has faced several such challenges in recent years. Those challenges have been exacerbated by the impact of mismanagement, corruption, nepotism and lack of rule of law, which have also led to disruptions in various sectors and a decline in economic activities.

The report by Verit Research highlights their concern about lack of action in Sri Lanka regarding blacklisting

contractors engaged in corrupt practices in public procurements. In contrast to other South Asian countries like Nepal and Bangladesh, which have hundreds of contractors blacklisted for corrupt activities, Sri Lanka has not blacklisted even a single contractor for such actions. This has led Sri Lanka to have the worst record among the South Asian countries in that regard.

Corruption has been a significant contributing factor to economic losses in Sri Lanka. Rampant corruption leads to misallocation of resources, undermines investor confidence, deters foreign investments and hampers economic growth. Corruption in government institutions and public services distorts market mechanisms, increases the cost of doing business and reduces overall economic efficiency.

Sir, farmers in Sri Lanka face numerous challenges, including lack of access to credit, fluctuating market prices, inadequate infrastructure and difficulties in obtaining quality inputs such as seeds and fertilizers. Additionally, unpredictable weather patterns and natural disasters pose risks to crop yields and livelihoods. Fishermen also encounter various issues, such as competition from industrial fishing vessels, overfishing, declining fish stocks and environmental degradation. Also lack of proper equipment, safety measures and market access further hinder the fishing industrys sustainable development.

Sir, the northern and the eastern parts of Sri Lanka have suffered immensely from the effects of the civil war, which occurred between 1983 and 2009, resulting in widespread displacement, infrastructure destruction and increased poverty. Even after the conflict ended, those regions still face economic challenges such as limited job opportunities and insufficient access to basic services, particularly affecting the agricultural sector. Social issues like limited education and access to healthcare further perpetuate poverty, affecting vulnerable groups like war-affected populations and marginalized communities.

Sir, rebuilding and recovering from the conflicts impact in the Northern and the Eastern regions is still ongoing, necessitating reconciliation efforts, infrastructure development and economic revitalization for sustainable progress. To address those challenges, targeted poverty alleviation programmes, improved infrastructure, access to markets, job creation, education, healthcare and social services are crucial. Strengthening community-based initiatives and ensuring the participation of marginalized groups in development efforts are essential for promoting sustainable development and enhancing the well-being of the population in those regions.

Sir, corruption remains a prevalent issue in Sri Lanka with reports of corrupt practices in government institutions, public services, procurement processes and other sectors. Corruption undermines the rule of law, erodes public trust in the Government and distorts market mechanisms. It hinders economic growth by increasing the cost of doing business, deterring foreign investments
