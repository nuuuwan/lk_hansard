Last time during the COVID-19 epidemic, there was forced cremation of victims as suggested by an expert committee, implementing an unscientific, racist policy. But still, no departmental inquiry has been conducted against that expert committee for having taken that unscientific decision or for having arrived at such a horrendous decision without any foundation, which caused serious trauma for the entire Muslim community in this country.

Now, my concern is this, Hon. Minister. I have copied to you that letter with the heading Concerns over the imminent appointment of the Chief Epidemiologist, Ministry of Health, which I tabled earlier. A member of that particular expert committee, which gave that unscientific proposal to cremate COVID victims, who had been in the Philippines has now come here and you are making him the Acting Chief Epidemiologist. Undoubtedly, he may be elevated to that position. Our community and other communities which had been impacted on and had been worried about this situation have now raised this issue. I am asking you, Hon. Minister, that they be subjected to, at least, a departmental inquiry for having taken that unscientific decision. This particular gentleman who has come from the Philippines, Dr. Hasitha Tissera, is being appointed to an acting position at this time while there are allegations against him. Publicly, the Government has apologized for taking that decision. Therefore, I request that that appointment be stopped.

Then, there are also others. Today, it has been reported in the newspapers that another member of that expert committee called Dr. Ruwanpura, JMO of the Karapitiya Teaching Hospital, has spoken about legalizing abortion. These people are taking all kinds of new stands now, whereas they had, without any basis, taken that unscientific decision which caused serious impact on the Muslim community in this country. Therefore, I am requesting you, Hon. Minister, since your Government has apologized for that decision, please take action not to appoint such officials to such important positions, particularly if there is a pandemic once again. Our community is seriously worried about this issue.

(a) It is observed in the examination of previous files that the agreement entered into by the Sri Lanka Cement Corporation with Asha Minerals (Pvt.) Ltd. was an agreement entered into without the consent/awareness of the Ministry of Industry, which was the Line Ministry at that time. Therefore, information was sought from the

Chairman of the Cement Corporation regarding the reasons and methods for entering into the said agreement and the said information was included in the Chairman's letter dated 05.08.2024 submitted as Annexure 01*.

Furthermore, even if it is stated in the letter of the Chairman of the Cement Corporation dated 05.08.2024 that the Sri Lanka Cement Corporation was informed by the Ministry of Industry by its letter dated 23.11.2022 to provide necessary facilities to Asha Minerals (Pvt.) Ltd. to extract the Puttalam ilmenite deposit, it is observed that the letter dated 23.11. 2022 of the Secretary of the Ministry of Industry submitted as Annexure 02* only states "A request made by Asha Minerals (Pvt.) Ltd. for obtaining samples is referred to the Sri Lanka Cement Corporation for further work".

(b) (i) The relevant letter is a letter addressed to the Secretary to the President by the Secretary of the Ministry of Industry, which was the Line Ministry at that time.

(ii) As per the appeal dated 19.10.2023 sent by

the Puttalam Ilmenite Limited to the Ministry of Finance, Economic Stabilization and National Policies, the relevant parties have been convened and given necessary recommendations on 30.01.2024 and 11.03.2024. The discussion notes containing the decisions reached and recommendations made at those discussions have been forwarded in writing to the Sri Lanka Cement Corporation on 05.02.2024 and 25.03.2024, respectively.

(iii) It is stated in the letter dated 05.08.2024 (Annexure 01) of the Chairman of Sri Lanka Cement Corporation that as per the instructions given by the Ministry of Finance, Economic Stabilization and National Policies to the Sri Lanka Cement Corporation on 30.01.2024 and 11.03.2024 respectively, the approval of the Board of Directors of Sri Lanka Cement Corporation had not been given "for handing over the Aruwakkalu limestone quarry owned by the Sri Lanka Cement Corporation without making any payment to the owner of the land and in a way that makes it a disadvantage to the Corporation and the Government of Sri Lanka.

(iv) Sri Lanka Cement Corporation is an entity with legal personality incorporated under the State Industrial Corporations Act, No. 49 of 1957. The Board of Directors is responsible for the overall performance of all its operations, management and control.
