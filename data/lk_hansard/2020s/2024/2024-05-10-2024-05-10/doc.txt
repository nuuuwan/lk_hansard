( ) (The Hon. Deputy Chairman of Committees) Next, the Hon. M.A. Sumanthiran. [1.45 pm.] .. ( .. ) (The Hon. M.A. Sumanthiran) Thank you, Hon. Deputy Chairman of Committees, for the opportunity given to say a few words.

I would speak only with regard to the Vote of Condolence of the Hon. Gamini Jayawickrama Perera today since I had known only him out of the three, about whom references are being made. Very quickly, I must record the fact that when the Hon. Jayawickrama Perera was the Minister in charge of wildlife in the last Parliament, he worked very closely with us, particularly with regard to degazetting areas which had been declared under the Department of Wildlife Conservation, the Department of Forest and various other agencies. In doing so, we had a difficulty because of the long years of war and displacement of people. He looked at it from a very humanitarian point of view, came to Jaffna, held meetings in various localities with the people and figured the actual situation, held a meeting at the Jaffna Kachcheri, directed the officials to redo the boundaries and enable the people to continue with their normal livelihood. That was a great act by the Minister at that time. When the officials delayed doing that, he invited me also to the Ministry and with me there, he pulled them up for the long delay in doing it. It is a matter of regret that even today, that has not been done under the current President. On 27th January, 2023, I am told, the Cabinet took a decision to go back to the 1985 boundary lines and that is yet to be realized. But, when the Hon. Gamini Jayawickrama Perera was Minister, he really pushed for that and tried to get it implemented.

I must also mention one other matter. When he was the Chief Minister of the first Provincial Council of Wayamba, he showed the rest of the country what provincial councils, even with their limited powers, could do. He enacted so many legislations including the Statute on environment, which is a classic example of how even a provincial legislation could override the Central Government legislation on that area. To date, it is the legislation that is in force. So, he showed us the way.

We asked for devolution of power and provincial councils were established consequent to the Indo-Lanka Accord. Unfortunately, at that time, the North-East Provincial Council had been dissolved, but in the rest of

So, for those two reasons, I wish to, on behalf of the people of the North and the East, record my condolences on the demise of the Hon. Gamini Jayawickrama Perera and request that these sentiments also be communicated to his family. Thank you.
