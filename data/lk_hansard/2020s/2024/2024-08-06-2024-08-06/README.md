Leader of the Opposition just mentioned. He mentioned that during the election time, some of his programmes have been suspended. The Election Commission has clearly announced that no such political promotion activities should take place. Obviously, I would commend him on his Sakwala programme, but I would like to answer just one particular matter he raised. As far as the projects of the Government go, the Election Commission was very clear that no political promotions should take place and that there should be no intent to promote a particular presidential candidate. As far as the election law goes, we have to keep in mind that the Government projects should continue as they have been continuing with no political promotion of the said presidential candidate. However, if any other presidential candidate utilizes funding from an unknown source that has not been vetted or verified, I think, everybody would have an issue.

With that being said, now, I would like to take up the matter of the salinity barrier. As the Minister of Water Supply and Estate Infrastructure Development, I must say that the salinity barrier had been constructed across the Nilwala River in Weragampita area with the aim of providing uninterrupted clean drinking water to approximately 800,000 people in parts of Matara and Hambantota Districts; that was the notion with which that barrier was built. The Hon. Buddhika Pathirana brought this issue to my attention and we had multiple meetings.

Again, I must clarify another statement made by the Hon. Leader of the Opposition, that 150,000 people are being affected by this. Yes, there are certain issues, which the Ministries are working on. We are working on those along with the help of the Hon. Members of the Opposition as well. However, I must remind everybody in this country of the necessity of providing clean drinking water to people. Around 150,000 people are affected. Yes, there may be an issue, but we can search for a solution. At the same time, that should not be an impediment or a reason to disregard the 800,000 people who require clean drinking water. We have not been able to achieve Sustainable Development Goal No. 6. Why? That is because of the issues which pertain to providing clean drinking water. Are you telling me that the very farmers and their families who are affected by this barrrier do not need clean drinking water? When it comes to a matter like this, there needs to be bipartisanship and collective responsibility, with which we can take the solution forward.

I would like to remind this House that when I became the Minister of Water Supply and Estate Infrastructure Development in 2023, I inherited this issue. From the time I have taken over this Ministry, we have been trying to look for a collective solution. However, we do not wish to make this into a circus; we are trying to see how we can come up with a solution. During the period of construction of this salinity barrier, a substantial portion of the river was covered by the cofferdam, impacting on about seven planting seasons. However, following the completion of the construction in September, 2022, farmers had been able to carry out their agricultural activities without flood threats during the Maha season in
