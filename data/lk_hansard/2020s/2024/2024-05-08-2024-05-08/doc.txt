[11.21 a.m.] ... ( ) ( ... - ) (The Hon. M.U.M. Ali Sabry, PC - Minister of Foreign Affairs) Hon. Deputy Speaker, I will just give an overview of what we are trying to do with these particular Orders under the Dipomatic Privileges Act, which we have published in the Gazette.

Sir, I seek approval of Parliament for the implementation of immunities and privileges agreed for the International Centre for Research in Agroforestry - ICRAF- and the Global Green Growth Institute - GGGI - and the Agreement to be acceded with the International Atomic Energy Agency - IAEA - on the Privileges and Immunities of the IAEA.

1039 1040

The Government of Sri Lanka, from time to time, enters into Status Agreements with intergovernmental, international and regional organizations to provide such organizations with certain privileges and immunities for their operation in Sri Lanka. The legal framework that facilitates these privileges and immunities is contained in Sections 4 and 5 of the Diplomatic Privileges Act, No. 9 of 1996. When the Government of Sri Lanka enters into agreements with international and intergovernmental organizations to provide for immunities and privileges, in order to give effect to those, the Minister of Foreign Affairs is supposed to publish such immunities and privileges by Order in the Gazette and place it before Parliament in terms of Section 4 and 5 of the Diplomatic Privileges Act, No. 9 of 1996. That is why we have published these Orders in the Gazette and also placed them before Parliament.

Given the above, Sri Lanka has signed Host Country Agreements with the ICRAF on 14th September, 2022 and with the GGGI, on 07th February, 2023. Sri Lanka also intends to accede to the Agreement on the Privileges and Immunities of the IAEA. That is, the International Atomic Energy Agency. In order to give effect to the provisions relating to privileges and immunities in the Agreements signed with ICRAF and the GGGI and the IAEA Agreement to be acceded to, I, Minister of Foreign Affairs, by Orders in Gazette Extraordinary No. 2348/48 dated 08th September, 2023 published the said provisions in keeping with Section 4 of the Diplomatic Privileges Act, No. 09 of 1996. Also, in keeping with Section 5 of the Diplomatic Privileges Act, No. 09 of 1996, the provisions relating to the privileges and immunities published by the Orders are now placed before Parliament for approval.

Why we have signed this Status Agreement with the ICRAF and given them the privileges, I will explain now. ICRAF operates as an international research centre for the purpose of improving and increasing sustainable and climate-resilient agricultural production throughout the developing world through research and related activities in such a way that the nutritional level and general economic well-being of low-income people are improved. ICRAF is headquartered in Nairobi, Kenya and has offices and presence in over 30 countries globally. ICRAF establishing a country office in Sri Lanka would expedite development of transformational projects in achieving a green economy, creating job opportunities for the rural and urban youth and improving management of soil, water and biological resources that would underpin the green economy in the country. A few of the current and proposed projects which would help achieving the above goals are,

(i) Sustainable management of the upper watershed for better management of erosion and water resources.

Approximately, a US Dollars 40 million GCF grant project was developed with the support of ICRAF together with the International Union for Conservation of Nature - IUCN. The executive entities for the GCF Knuckles Project are, ICRAF

and the Ministry of Irrigation and the project is implemented in partnership with several other key Ministries, including the Ministry of Environment as the National Designated Authority for GCF.

ii. Development of four transformational projects under the Readiness Grant including an estimated US Dollars 150 million project that aims to develop export value chains based on improved varieties of under-invested tree crops such as jack, kithul et cetera.

iii. Capacity strengthening and technology transfer to key government and research entities in genomics, landscape and spatial analysis and monitoring, evidence-based - big data - decision-making and transformational change are some of the benefits which we will receive when we get the ICRAF to set up their office in Sri Lanka.

The GGGI is a treaty-based international, intergovernmental organization that is dedicated to supporting and promoting strong, inclusive and sustainable economic growth in developing countries and emerging economies. Its mission is to support members in the transformation of their economies into a green growth economic model. The GGGI plays the role of an enabler and facilitator for the transition of its member countries into low-carbon green economies by providing policy and technical support in the development of green growth plans, support in mobilization of green investments, implementation of green growth projects with identified key national entities and development of local capacities and knowledge sharing.

The GGGI has a Framework Agreement with the Green Climate Fund - GCF - for their Readiness Programme. The GCF, the Green Climate Fund, is the largest fund in the world in relation to climate change. At present, the GGGI is the delivery partner for the GCF National Adaptation Planning Readiness Support Project and is working closely with the Ministry of Environment on revising Sri Lanka's National Adaptation Plan - NAP - for 2025-2034 and preparing Sri Lankas first set of Provincial Adaptation Plans - PAPs - and investment plans for all nine provinces in Sri Lanka.

Accordingly, partnering with the GGGI will accrue the following benefits to Sri Lanka: obtain assistance required to implement the strategies, methodologies and plans in line with the green growth activities and to help secure financial assistance as well as other investment facilities necessary for the purpose. Hon. Deputy Speaker, it will also help us gain access to projects, for which financial assistance is provided through the GCF Readiness support as well as other international funds and to formulate acceptable project proposals. It will also help us secure support for capacity development of State sector in the context of green growth and climate action and an opportunity to exchange knowledge, tools, and methodologies relevant to green development and climate change among member countries.

In addition to being the delivery partner on the revision of the National Adaptation Plan, the ongoing initiatives in Sri Lanka by the GGGI will include

facilitating the adoption of sustainable public transport services - e-mobility - for low-carbon city development in the Colombo Metropolitan area and exploration of renewable energy investment opportunities. So, those are two very important institutions which are setting up their Country Offices in Sri Lanka. In terms of our obligation, we are providing them with certain privileges and immunities so that they would be comfortable here in dealing with the Government and the agencies of the Government.

Why are we giving these privileges and immunities to the IAEA? The IAEA is the worlds intergovernmental forum for scientific and technical co-operation to secure peaceful and safe use of nuclear science and technology and nuclear power, which contributes to the UN Sustainable Development Goals.

The IAEA was established as an autonomous international organization under the UN system on 29th July, 1957 and Sri Lanka has been a founding member since 1957. Though established independently of the United Nations, through its own international Treaty, the IAEA reports to both the United Nations General Assembly and the Security Council and is headquartered in Vienna, Austria.

The Agreement on Privileges and Immunities of the IAEA defines the legal capacity and the status of the IAEA, the officials, international experts and delegates of member States who would attend various IAEA workshops, meetings, trainings and expert missions conducted in Sri Lanka.

The programmes of the IAEA encourage the development of peaceful application of nuclear energy, science and technology, safeguard against misuse of nuclear technology and nuclear materials and promote nuclear safety and security standards including radiation protection and their implementation.

Hon. Deputy Speaker, it has become difficult to get IAEA experts and organize training courses, meetings and workshops in Sri Lanka as Sri Lanka is not a State Party to the Agreement on Privileges and Immunities of the IAEA. So, the following would be the expected benefits of becoming a State Party - we are doing it today - to the Agreement on Privileges and Immunities of the IAEA: (a) provide and receive credible assurance regarding the peaceful nature of nuclear programmes; (b) enhance transparency, build confidence and facilitate peaceful nuclear cooperation; (c) keep control over nuclear materials and nuclear activities conducted in Sri Lanka under the control of the Government by adopting a system that accounts for and controls nuclear materials and (d) demonstrate Sri Lankas strong commitment to the global non-proliferation regime.
