() : CODE OF CRIMINAL PROCEDURE (AMENDMENT) BILL:

DETERMINATION OF THE SUPREME COURT

( ) (The Hon. Speaker) I wish to announce that I have received the Decision of the Supreme Court in respect of the Bill titled Code of Criminal Procedure (Amendment), which had been challenged in the Supreme Court in terms of Article 121 (1) of the Constitution.

The Supreme Court, having considered the submissions, has held that there was no material to conclude that any of the provisions of the Bill violates any provision of the Constitution and accordingly, the Petitioner had informed the Court that the Petition will be withdrawn. Hence the Supreme Court had allowed to withdraw the Petition, terminated further proceedings and dismissed the Petition.

I order that the Decision of the Supreme Court be printed in the Official Report of todays Proceedings of the House.

GAZETTE NOTIFICATION NO. 2430/2025 ON SETTLEMENT OF

LAND

[5.14 p.m.]

.. ( .. ) (The Hon. G.G. Ponnambalam) Hon. Presiding Member, I move the following Adjournment Motion:

By Gazette Notification bearing number 2430/2025 dated 28.03.2025, settlement notices totalling seven in number (Numbered from 5617 to 5623) for the Districts of Jaffna, Mullaitivu, Kilinochchi and Mannar covering more than 5,000 acres has been published.

The said notifications attempt to take over land including beaches, lakes, cart lanes and cemeteries - land that was used by the Tamil people from time immemorial as res communes - and convert them into State land.

The Tamil struggle for self-determination originated inter alia in response to the alienation of State land by the Sri Lankan State with the explicit intent of changing the demographics of the North-East, through which the Sri Lankan State attempts and continues to attempt to make the Tamils a minority in their own homeland. The Tamils have consistently maintained that their autonomous institutions must have control over State land in the North-East.

The above Gazette Notification is another initiative in the long history of depriving the Tamils of their homeland by successive Sri Lankan Governments.

Furthermore, our understanding is that the land sought to be declared includes private lands which those private owners have difficulty establishing ownership owing to loss of documentation during the tsunami and repeated displacements.

This Government in its manifesto, both at the Presidential Elections of 2024 and the General Elections of 2025, promised to genuinely explore a constitutional settlement to the National Question which includes settling the question of who controls State land. However, this act on the part of the Government makes clear that their views are no different to the previous governments in relation to control of the State land. The Tamil people see this action on the part of the Government as yet another act of structural genocide of the Tamil nation.

I urge that the Government immediately withdraw this Gazette Notification.

Sir, I am the Mover of this Motion, but you have given me only six minutes.

Hon. Minister, with the motives you have just stated, we would have no problems. But, the law you are trying to use has the completely opposite intention. So, if your intention is actually not to pronounce the lands you have demarcated, those 6,000 acres, as State land, this is not the law to use. What we are suggesting is what you have suggested doing with regard to land for wildlife and forestry. You appoint a committee or a commission, we will cooperate.

We accept that there are severe problems with regard to land, which need to be sorted out. Because of displacements, multiple displacements, and the tsunami, people do not have documents. That has to be resolved. We will cooperate with you, but please do not use this land. I do not know who has advised you, Hon. Minister. But, this land are going to be acquired to make them State land; it is to declare them State land. You talk about suspending this Gazette, but you cannot suspend it. Once the Gazette is issued by the 28th of June, this land will vest as State land, if they are not claimed. You cannot suspend the Gazette. I do not know on what legal advice you are making this position in this House. You cannot suspend it; you have to revoke it.

And, I assure you, Hon. Minister, that you will have our cooperation in sorting out the problems of our people. I mean, why would we not want our peoples problems be solved, whether it is your Government or any other government in power? But, I am telling you with utmost responsibility, you are using the wrong law to do that. On the contrary, this law, by the 28th of June, will declare all those 6,000-odd acres you have demarcated as State land. So, I kindly urge you to withdraw this Gazette. I assure you that all the Hon. Members seated on this side of the House belonging to the North-East, the Tamil Members, will cooperate with you. It is in our interest to solve the

land issues which have been plaguing our people because they have lost documents. So, we understand the issue. But, this is not the way - you are frightening us and not even the Rajapaksas did this - to go about it.

And, if you actually want to solve this problem, you should have had a word with us. You have given just three months! If we did not bring this out into the open, those private citizens who are supposed to make claims to these land would not have even known about this. You tell me, what is the effort you made in order to draw public awareness to this Gazette? If we did not make it a controversy, nobody would have known about it! So, kindly understand, this is not the way to go about it. You are frightening us. You must withdraw this Gazette. We will cooperate with you; if the intention is actually to solve the problems, you can be assured that we would cooperate.
