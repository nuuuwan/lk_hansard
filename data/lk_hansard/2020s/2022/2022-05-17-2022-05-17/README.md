"That the proceedings on item No. 1 of Public Business appearing on the Order Paper be exempted at this days sitting from the provisions of Standing Order 27 of Parliament."

People might be wondering what that is. That is a Motion of Censure that has been forwarded to Parliament since Article 42 of the Constitution states, I quote:

The President shall be responsible to Parliament for the due exercise, performance and discharge of his powers, duties and functions under the Constitution and any written law, including the law for the time being relating to public security.

Sir, this is a Motion of Censure, a Motion expressing what people have been expressing out in the country. I do not want to use any slogans, but they have been asking the President to step down. But, as Representatives of the people, this is an opportunity for us to reflect, to mirror what is being said by the country at large. So, that is the Motion which is referred to in the Motion I am now moving. That is in the Order Paper as Item No. 1 under Notice of Motions at the commencement of Public Business and it is moved that it be taken up today.

All that will happen is, we can find out whether this Parliament agrees with the large majority of the country or not. That is all that will happen. So, while I move this Motion, I urge the Members of Parliament not to block this discussion in Parliament today because this is the discussion that is happening in the country today. If this Parliament is a representative body of the country today, please allow this discussion to take place in Parliament. We do not have to shout at each other. We can discuss it and then decide whether, as Representatives of the people, we agree or do not agree with what is being said outside. So, my fervent appeal to my Colleagues in this House is not to block that substantive Motion of Censure being discussed in Parliament today. Thank you. ( ) (The Hon. Speaker) .
