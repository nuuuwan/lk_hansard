. ( . ) (The Hon. Premnath C. Dolawatte, Attorney-at-Law) Thank you, Hon. Speaker.

Sir, I wish to bring to your notice a breach of Powers and Privileges of Parliament arising from the interim order made on Friday, 3rd March, 2023 in the Supreme Court Case SC (FR) Application No. 69/2023.

69 70

Sir, Parliament has the power to act when there is a violation of its Powers and Privileges arising from Article 4 (c) of the Constitution.

Articles 148, 149 and 150 of the Constitution vest Parliament with firstly, the full control over Public Finance including the Consolidated Fund; secondly, monies from the Consolidated Fund can only be withdrawn under a warrant signed by the Minister of Finance; and thirdly, a warrant can be issued only if the money is voted by Parliament for a specific public purpose. -[Interruption.]

Parliament, by Appropriation Act, No. 43 of 2022, has allocated monies for activities of the Government for the financial year 2023 from the taxes, imposts, rates, duties and all other revenues received by the Consolidated Fund. Section 7 of the same Act empowers the Minister to limit expenditure previously authorized if the receipt of revenue is less than anticipated.

Furthermore, Section 3 of the Fiscal Management (Responsibility) Act, No. 3 of 2003 requires the Government to adhere to the following objectives:

(b) prudent management of the financial risks faced by the government, having regard to the changing economic circumstances ;

Under Sections 10, 11 and 12 of the Act, the Minister in charge of the subject of Finance has to inform Parliament if there is a shortfall in estimated revenue or cash flow in the Mid-Year Fiscal Position Report.

Under Article 43(1) read with Article 148 of the Constitution, the Minister is responsible to Parliament which controls public finance in giving effect to these laws and financial regulations. The interim order issued by several judges of the Supreme Court attempts to prevent the implementation of these laws and make the control of public finance by Parliament a nullity. It is also an interference with the sacrosanct powers vested in Parliament in respect of the control of public finances. The interim order seeks to give effect to the Activity Budget Estimates for the year 2023. The Activity Budget Estimates is not a law nor a legal document. It is a summary of the Appropriation Bill for 2023 to assist Parliament during the Debate. This interim order seeks to give legal validity to the Activity Budget Estimates overriding an Act of Parliament, that is, the Appropriation Act, No. 43 of 2022. This will also affect the prudent management of public finance thereby violating the provisions of the Fiscal management (Responsibility) Act.

Under the Constitution, the Election Commission is responsible to Parliament. The Chairman of the Election Commission has already communicated to you, Hon. Speaker, the issues faced by the Commission in respect of the Local Authorities Election. Furthermore, a Resolution has been introduced into the Order Paper to appoint a Select Committee to inquire into the holding of the said Election by the Election Commission. Therefore, these are matters which have already come within the purview of Parliament and are now matters that constitute

Business of Parliament. This matter was brought to the notice of Court by the Attorney Generals Department before the interim order was made and it appears that the Judges who issued that order has overlooked the same.

A number of applications were filed in the Supreme Court regarding the Local Authorities Election 2023. The proceedings in respect of these applications are pending before another bench of the Supreme Court. In those proceedings, one of the issues pending determination of the Supreme Court is the Secretary to the Treasurys affidavit on non-availability of funds. The counsel for the Elections Commission has sought further time from court to respond to the said affidavit. A similar affidavit was filed in this application. The interim order has disregarded this affidavit, the contents of which is under consideration by the other bench. When one bench of the Supreme Court has proceeded to disregard this fact and issue a stay order, it would constitute a contravention of established practices and norms leading to judicial anarchy.

The Election Commission is not a party to this application - SC (FR) Application No. 69/2023. The interim order has been made without any reference to the Election Commission.

The interim order violates principle of natural justice. One of the learned Judges who issued the interim order is related to a petitioner in a similar case being heard before the other bench of the Supreme Court. The learned Judge has not disclosed the relationship nor has he recused himself from the case.

Hon. Speaker, I recall my memory of the Late Hon. Anura Banaranaikes decision over the Court.

Therefore, I see this is a clear breach of privilege and power of Parliament. Please refer this to the Committee on Ethics and Privileges as an urgent matter.
