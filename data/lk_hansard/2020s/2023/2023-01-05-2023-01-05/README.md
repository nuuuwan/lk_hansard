[2.34 p.m.] .. ( .. ) (The Hon. M.A. Sumanthiran) Hon. Presiding Member, the Hon. Minister of Justice, Prison Affairs and Constitutional Reforms has moved the Second Reading of the Bill titled Recovery of Possession of Premises Given on Lease. More than the Bill that he has moved, it is the Bill that he did not move which is of concern. Nevertheless, I just want say something on the Bill that has been moved.

In our experience, it is State institutions which have, in the past, entered into lease agreements with persons who are friendly with the government of the day and those lease agreements did not have a termination clause. It was deliberately done. No self-respecting lawyer or a

notary will prepare a lease agreement which does not have a termination clause, but the UDA did that. And so, this is a welcome move, that even if no termination clause is there, it can now, under the law, be terminated for default and so on.

The Bill that he did not move is the Bureau of Rehabilitation Bill. The Government seems to have some kind of obsession with this Bureau they want to create. The Supreme Court, this time around, has made an excellent Determination on that Bill. I have said this in this House before also in relation to that particular Determination. This Determination, like one other Determination in the past which I am aware of - the Determination on the Sri Lanka Broadcasting Authority Bill - specifically states that that Bill, as a whole, violates the Constitution. Nevertheless, since it is their constitutional duty to specify what provisions need a two-thirds majority, what provisions need a Referendum in addition and how those can be remedied, the court has made certain suggestions. And the Government, although the Bill has been looked upon as something that is obnoxious to the rule of law and constitutionalism, still wants to pursue it. So, that is the concern. Even today, the Minister did not withdraw the Bill. I am glad the Hon. Minister is now coming into the Chamber. The Minister only put it off and has said that they will take certain concerns into consideration before finalizing that Bill.

One of the important concerns we have is, as to who constitutes this Bureau? It cannot be the military. If you want to rehabilitate drug dependents, I do not understand what the military or the security officials have to do with that. That has to be done by experts in that area. It is not an easy task to rehabilitate drug dependents. The Supreme Court has very specifically struck down different categories of persons to be subjected to this rehabilitation and said that for drug dependents, it can or any other person identified by law, meaning that such a person, today, should have been identified by law, not for the Committee Stage amendments to state "Drug dependents or any other persons to be identified by law". That is keeping the whole field open, which is what you wanted to do in the first place and which is what had been disallowed by court. Therefore, the constitutionality of that phrase is a serious question. I say that that cannot be enacted in that way; that may be violating the caution expressed by the supreme court.

There is one other fundamental issue I want to address to the Hon. Minister. When a person is deprived of his personal liberty, that must happen only after a judicial pronouncement. Now, this is a departure from that norm; here, you are envisaging a situation where, without the court finding a person guilty of certain things, that person can be "incarcerated". I am using that word advisedly because he is being deprived of his personal liberty.

There are provisions in the Bill which state that if he runs away, you can catch him and bring him back. The one important fundamental in a drug rehabilitation programme is the voluntariness of the person, that the person must want to be rehabilitated; you cannot force it down his throat. So, in addition to a judicial

pronouncement, I also say, in a matter where drug dependency is concerned, you must also have a medical opinion. With both those conditions attached, then you can set up a rehabilitation centre, but it must be administered and run by experts in that field, not by security experts.
