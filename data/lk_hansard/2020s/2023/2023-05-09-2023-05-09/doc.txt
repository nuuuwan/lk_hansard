(ii) Approximately, a total number of 92,435 Sri Lankan

Tamils are living inside the refugee camps and outside the camps in Tamil Nadu.

If the Sri Lankans living inside the camps under the camp registration want to return to Sri Lanka, they have to submit a request to the camp authorities for their willingness to voluntary return.

The District Collector Office will forward the request and other details of the Sri Lankans to the Q Branch, local police station and UNHCR.

The relevant authorities will verify the details and submit their report to the District Collector.

Based on the above reports, the necessary exit permit will be granted by the District Collector which is valid for six months from the date of issue.

2023 09

After receiving the exit permit from the District Collector, the exit visa application has to be applied online for each individual through the FRRO - Foreigners Regional Registration Office - online portal and upload the necessary documents. (Camp identity card is a mandatory document.)

The FRRO will analyze the application and the necessary exit visa will be granted without any penalty.

The Sri Lankans who are living outside camps could also return to Sri Lanka after directly approaching the UNHCR or at their own expense. They also need to apply online individually and obtain exit visa through the FRRO's - Foreigners Regional Registration Office's - online portal and upload the necessary documents.

(iii) If the Sri Lankan is not under the camp registration, they can directly approach the UNHCR or travel at their own expense.

The exit visa application has to be applied online for each individual through the FRRO - Foreigners Regional Registration Office - online portal and upload the necessary documents.

During the application to the FRRO, the Sri Lankan has to submit the proof of camp identity card which was issued to them on their initial arrival by boat. If this document is submitted, the penalty will not be reflected in the FRRO portal.

If the Sri Lankan fails to submit the arrival camp identity card or if the Sri Lankan came by flight and overstayed, the penalty amount will be reflected in the FRRO portal.

The Government of India has given an option to waive the penalty for the Sri Lankans.

For the waiver, the Sri Lankans have to submit their appeal to the Department of Rehabilitation of the Government of Tamil Nadu with all supportive documents.

The Rehabilitation Department will forward the request to Q Branch, FRRO and UNHCR and relevant reports will be in turn submitted by them.

A committee will be formed and all the waiver applications will be analysed and the respective waiver maybe granted.

If the waiver is granted, the Sri Lankan can apply for the exit visa online on the FRRO portal and the necessary visa will be granted without penalty.

The FRRO analyses the application and the necessary exit visa will be granted without any penalty.

(b) Approximately, a total number of 7,931 refugees have returned to Sri Lanka since 2012 to date under the UNHCR Refugee Repatriation Programme - RRP - and with the assistance of Sri Lanka Deputy High Commission in Chennai.

If any Sri Lankans living inside the refugee camps or outside the refugee camps in Tamil Nadu want to return to Sri Lanka voluntarily through the UNHCR or at their own expense, the Deputy High Commission of Sri Lanka facilitates their repatriation to Sri Lanka by issuing one-way travel document (one-way passport), free of charge.

(c) The above are the general procedures for a Sri Lankan expatriate to voluntarily return to Sri Lanka. However, this procedure may slightly vary on case-by-case basis.

The Sri Lanka Deputy High Commission has informed that the repatriation to Sri Lanka through UNHCR was suspended from May, 2022 to January, 2023. However, the repatriation process has resumed since February, 2023 and totally 18 Sri Lankan refugees have been repatriated to Sri Lanka through UNHCR from 14.02.2023 to 28.02.2023.

Furthermore, two batches of UNHCR repatriation had been scheduled on 16.03.2023 and 21.03.2023, through which 18 Sri Lankans were expected to travel to Sri Lanka. By the time we were preparing the Answer, they had come to Sri Lanka.

In addition, a committee has been appointed by the Secretary to the President to facilitate and expeditiously carry out the process of repatriation of Sri Lankans who have been staying in India as refugees during the recent two, three decades.
