Hon. Presiding Member, the Hon. President came to this House in the morning and announced the grant of the second tranche by the IMF in our debt restructuring process. According to the President, the next step would be to renegotiate with the private creditors, which is also quite a challenging task. In the meantime, it is his contention that the contraction of the economy is a serious challenge we are faced with at the moment.

If you go by the Y ala and Maha seasons and the harvest that we have been managing to produce, we see that there is a sharp drop and that this trend is going to continue. What is connected with this is, the climate vulnerabilities in the country. Climate-related disasters is a serious problem that our country very frequently faces, as we are a country close to the equator. Being a very tropical country, we are now faced with very frequent climate-related disasters.

If you take flooding, the recent flooding in the Akurana area is a phenomenon that we see happening very frequently. If we take the flooding of the Pinga-Oya which flows through the Akurana Town towards Katugastota and merges with the Mahaweli River, there was an assumption that the erection of the Polgolla Dam is what is causing all these issues and that the sand silting collected at the place where the Oya merges with the Mahaweli River has got its flow affected due to the Polgolla Dam. The fact is, we had had several attempts made to solve this issue. There had been an inter-

ministerial committee chaired by a Senior Assistant Secretary of my own Ministry and we also appointed a task force to go into the reasons for flooding of the Pinga-Oya across the Akurana Town very frequently. Four months ago, at the meeting held in the Akurana Divisional Secretariat, I raised the issue and asked the Divisional Secretary and the officials of the UDA who had come there as to whether any action has been taken about the unauthorized structures which had to be demolished according to the report that was submitted. So, follow-up action is what is lacking together with various other connected improvements that we have to make along the Pinga-Oya flow. From about the eighth or the ninth mile post up to the Ambatale Junction, there have been a variety of issues which need to be resolved. This needs both certain legal actions by authorities like the Urban Development Authority, which had been quite lacking in its approach to taking those actions, and also a civil society responsibility to engage with those agencies to see that it is expedited. So, this matter, though we had raised it several times and only four months ago I raised it at the District Development Committee meeting, too, is still pending. Therefore, I, in fact, brought it up here since it is the President who is the Minister of Finance and his Vote is being debated today. I made a request from the President to allow us to have a meeting with him in Kandy when he visits Kandy this Sunday to participate in the District Development Committee meeting. He has assured both the Hon. Abdul Haleem and myself representing the Kandy District and other civil society representatives that after the District Development Committee meeting, we could meet him along with the officials of the UDA and other government agencies - the Department of Irrigation, the Disaster Management Centre and the Road Development Authority - who are to be summoned to discuss this matter. With that meeting, I hope we could take some effective action and the State agencies which are entrusted with the responsibility to carry out the necessary corrective work must do their work so that such disasters do not happen repeatedly all the time. I thank the Hon. President for having allowed us some time to meet him and discuss this issue.

Also, I want to refer to the Climate Resilience Improvement Project - CRIP. There must be some way in which the CRIP gets involved in this. I think that is a rolling project which has to be given more funding so that this type of frequent climate-related issues could be addressed effectively. Thank you.
