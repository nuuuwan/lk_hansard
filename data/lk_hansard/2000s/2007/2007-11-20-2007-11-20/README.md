In my view, His Excellency Mahinda Rajapaksa broke away from this conception of personalization of political power and converted the institution into a popular and acceptable organ of Government here in this country. He, as the fifth incumbent of this powerful office, the powerful Executive Presidency, gave it a new outlook, a popular outlook through which he tried to make it more

He opened the doors of the Presidency to all varieties of people including school children, school children from the Eastern Province as well. This, I describe, as one of the major innovations made during the last two years. He, through a large number of development programmes, showed the people that Presidential leadership is not insulated from the people, not far removed from the people. His Excellency Mahinda Rajapaksa, largely because of his background and his belief in the rights of the ordinary man in the village, injected certain tendencies into the office of the Presidency with a view to making it more close to the hearts of the ordinary man in the street. Poya day religious ceremonies and other religious activities at the Temple Trees amply demonstrate the extent to which the culture and religion has had an affect on the role of the Executive Presidency. Though they may be populist in character, though they may be populist in their outlook, one can say that His Excellency Mahinda Rajapaksa, unlike his predecessors in the high office, is one who understands the significance of the political culture of the country.
