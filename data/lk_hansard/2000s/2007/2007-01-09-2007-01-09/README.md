Hon. Deputy Speaker, may I take this opportunity towish you and all the Members of the House a Happy NewYear. With that sentiment, the Sri Lanka Muslim Congresswishes to make its contribution in this Debate to extendthe state of Emergency once again. At the very outset, Iwould like to remind the Government that we proposeto support this resolution on the condition that theextension of the state of Emergency should be used strictlyfor the purposes for which it is being promulgated.

We have seen some controversial incidents in the recentpast relating to issues of media freedom, issues pertainingto violation of human rights of the common people of thiscountry and we hope the Government would be quitesensitive to the concerns of parties like ours which havesome serious reservations about the way in which theEmergency Regulations are being used to protect thesovereignty of this country. It is very apt, I believe Mr.Deputy Speaker.

Today I read a news item on page four of the DailyMirror newspaper. This news item is attributed to the Hon.Minister of Cultural Affairs and National Heritage, the Hon.Mahinda Yapa Abeywardena. He says, 2007 namedNational year of drama. He, of course, is referring to theupliftment of the artistes and their famed skills in thiscountry. But when you go to look at variouspronouncements that are being made from the Governmentside we sure will be in for a year of drama. We have seenthe untold difficulties that the common man is undergoingtoday. If both the husband and wife are working and theyare to take the public transport, both of them do not traveltogether these days. They want at least one breadwinneralive if something unforeseen happens. For those peoplein the Northern and Eastern Provinces and many other partsof the bordering districts travelling to Colombo and other

127128

[ ]areas are fraught with so much of difficulties. They willhave to alight from the bus several times over, open theirbags and subject themselves to examinations and theirjourney is delayed by several hours.

We see many traffic snarls in the city and valuable manhours are being lost. The tourism industry is in thedoldrums. The economy is facing a very bleak prospectin this year. This being the case, we certainly are in fora National year of drama. In this midst, parties like oursare also living in hope of the impossible happening. Thehappenings in the APRC and the All Party Conference willbe a meaningless exercise, if we are to prusue a resolutionto the national ethnic problem in the manner in which weare proceeding these days. We would appeal to theGovernment to exercise restraint.

It is good for the Government to bear in mind that inthe Eastern Province LTTE never won battles andestablished camps. It has always been when the GovernmentForces themselves vacated the camps LTTE came andestablished themselves in those camps. I represent theEastern Province and I have been in this House from 1994.It was the previous administration after the failed talks in1995 that voluntarily vacated many camps in the EasternProvince. It was in that backdrop that the LTTE was ableto establish some control over those areas where they hadbrought large sections of people under their control. Ofcourse, there were some far-flung areas which had someLTTE activity. But very many places that came under thecontrol of the LTTE was not due to the fighting skills ofthe LTTE to capture territory, particularly in the EasternProvince. In the North, it is a different kettle of fish. Theywon territory by engaging in conventional war. But in theEastern Province no such massive military operations wereconducted in order to win over Government Security Forcecamps. They were all abandoned by the Governmentvoluntarily in order to capture territory and to strengthenthe forces in the Northern Province. Therefore, recapturingthe East even if the impossible happens is not going tobe a surprise. But in the meantime, there are otherresponsibilities cast on the Government.

It is my bounden duty to bring it to the notice of theGovernment. This is why we say that this year, 2007 isgoing to be a national year of drama. Mr. Deputy Speaker,it is very pertinent to read the article published in themiddle page of the Daily Mirror newspaper of todaytitled Rajapaksa bent on dictated peace . This article iswritten by none other than a former head of the IndianIntelligence Service, Research and Analysis Wing, Mr. B.Raman. There is a very pertinent statement he makes here.

It states, I quote :The fighters of the anti-LTTE faction headed byKaruna are now openly assisting the Army in itsiperations in the Eastern Province. No effort is made

any longer to conceal the presence and key role ofthe followers of Karuna in the military operationsin the Eastern Province.

This of course, is purely a military matter regarding whichthe SLMC does not want to make a comment. But, if itimpinges on the security of the innocent civilian populationthen it is our duty to bring it to the notice of theGovernment. We see today TMVP offices being openedvery adjacent to security forces camps. Several of thosecamps have come up in the Eastern Province. It is not amatter of secrecy any more as Mr. Raman states in thisreport. The Government is not endeavouring to distanceitself. Of course, there are other preposterous and may besubstantiated allegations that are being brought aboutregarding child conscription and assistance by Sri Lankanarmed forces for that activity which again is a matter leftfor the Government to defend. But, then, in many areasthe law and order machinery of the Government is turninga blind eye to the activities of the Karuna group when itaffects the civilians in many areas where they haveestablished their camps. The Muslim community remaineda very neutral party in the entire phase of hostilities notonly now but even in the past. There have been attemptsto drag the Muslim community into the hostilities. Theyhave suffered immensely for being neutral. They have beenmassacred in large numbers in villages. But, yet they neverdid anything overtly to deter the struggle of any communityin this country. Sinhalese civilians have been affected.Like the Muslims in many areas in the Eastern Province,Sinhalese have suffered. As a matter of fact, in theBatticaloa District alone 23 Muslim villages have had tobe vacated because of LTTE activities.
