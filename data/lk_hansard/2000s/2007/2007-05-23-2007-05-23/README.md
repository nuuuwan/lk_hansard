I want to mention a few things. One is, about the appointment of programme assistants. Sir, there were certain officers such as co-operative inspectors, rural development officers and so forth. They were graduates but they were paid a very poor salary. They were paid according to their GCE (Advanced Level) qualifications. So since they wanted to further their employment or enhance their salaries, they got into the programme assistants' service. But after going there they found that they were not fitting in because they have their experiences, they have passed their efficiency bar examinations, they have undergone various trainings in relation to their earlier field of employment, and they wanted to get back to their original positions. Certain officers were allowed but

appeals of certain officers were rejected. They wanted to get back to their earlier positions. You must consider this, Sir, because they joined this service hoping to get better salaries and facilities. Now they feel that their decision was wrong. So, if you think their services are useful to the Government and the officers concerned too would benefit, please consider their request without strictly following the Establishments Code. The higher officers do not study these cases adequately. So, please look into this favourably. Secondly, I want to say something about the pensioners. Now you are paying Rs. 1,750 as a cost of living allowance to all Government servants. For the pensioners only 50 per cent of it is paid. So, please consider paying the pensioners 100 per cent. About this ethnic proportion, I do not know the correct word. When you take in people to the Government service it is done on a percentage basis. There is a circular on this. That is, a certain percentage of Sinhalese, Tamils and Muslims are recruited. This is not adhered to now. A few months ago, Sri Lanka Agricultural Service competitive examination was held and not a single from the Tamil medium was selected. This means we will not have Sri Lanka Agricultural Service officers in our area. These people are going to be the research officers, lecturers supervising the extension service and so on. So, in the long run we will have no officers. Earlier, if I remember right, the Sri Lanka Administrative Service had a special examination and recruited about 40 SLAS officers for the North-East Province. Because of the Official Language Act many of our Tamil officers have left their jobs and gone abroad. They retired on their own. So, there is a severe dearth of Tamil government officers. Even the North-East Provincial Council is run by the senior citizens. What I mean is, they are all retired officers. So, kindly look into this and follow this circular when you recruit people for Government Service. Other than this, I was talking about the salaries. When I was a government officer I was paid only Rs. 200 but I was able to buy two gold sovereigns. But now I do not think that could be done. . The present Director of the Pensions Department is a very nice man, very sympathetic towards the government officers. But there is a problem, Sir. The Director of Pensions cannot do anything unless the other departments co-operate with him and feed all the information to him. So we have to sort it out and do something because earlier also there were a number of circulars and newspaper reports to pay the pension to the Government servants soon after they retire. But I know of cases where for more than three to four years they have not been paid their pensions. They are all Government servants who were living on their salary - a hand to mouth existence. They are all worrying me because I was a Government servant. They are coming to me and worrying me always. Hon. Minister, you have to find some solution to this problem also.
