Fiscal Policy Circular No. 01/2005 To : All Secretaries to Ministries Concessions on the payment of Value Added Tax on donations/purchases of goods and services by organizations or persons engage in the rehabilitiation work in Tsunami affected areas : 01. Foreign Donors In terms of the provisions of Value added Tax Act, following concessions can be provided on Value Added Tax (VAT) payable by International Donor Agencies who have entered into Memorandum of Understandings with the Government of Sri Lanka. A Exemption from payment of Value Added Tax Hon. Minister of Finance can grant a approval for VAT exemption for the import of goods donated by persons or organizations abroad and local purchases of goods funded directly by foreign organizations to be used in projects for the relief/rehabilitation and reconstruction of Tsunami disaster. Accordingly, in order to obtain the VAT concession, the Secretary of the respective Line Ministry should recommend the project including required goods in quantities to be purchased for such projects with the names of the suppliers. B. Deferment of payment of Value Added Tax on Local Purchases using foreign funds A system on deferment of Value Added Tax on local purchases has been introduced for the following activities in terms of sub paragraph (b) of subsection (2) of section (2) of the Value Added Tax Act. Supply of goods (raw materials or finished goods) or services locally, by a VAT registered person to any direct contractor (local or foreign institution assigned by the Foreign Donor Agency).

Accordingly, when a direct contractor awards a contract to a local supplier, on completion of the transaction, the supplier will issue an invoice to the direct contractor. Such invoices should be issued in the name of the project on behalf of the Line Ministry. Based on such invoices submitted by direct contractor, the Government of Sri Lanla will pay the VAT portion on the agreed contract value. As per above, the following scheme will be implemented to defer the VAT payable. (i) The Foreign Funded Donor Agency will inform the names and addresses of the direct contractors assigned for the work to be carried out, to the Fiscal Policy Department, Ministry of Finance & Planning. (ii) Direct contractors (or the Donor Agency, in the case of Donor Agency and the direct contractors are the same) should submit the project report to the Fiscal Policy Department with the recommendation of Line Ministry. Such project reports should include estimated cost of the project, quantity of goods and services to be purchased and the names of the suppliers. (iii) The Department of Fiscal Policy will inform the Department of Inland Revenue of the approved contracts. (vi) A Letter will be issued to the direct contractor by the Commissioner General, Department of Inland Revenue, based on the approval granted by the Minister of Finance to obtain Value Added tax deferment for local purchase of goods/services under this scheme. (v) After the goods and services are provided locally, the Tax Invoices issued to direct contractor should be forwarded to the Fiscal Policy Department, with a schedule on quarterly basis, by the direct contractor (vi) Department of Fiscal Policy will check the Tax Invoices with the original estimate consideration and if satisfied, make a fund transfer against each Value Added Tax registered supplier, to the Department of Inland Revenue and recover the funds allocated to such line Ministry on periodical basis. 02. Local Donor organizations Local Donor organizations who have engaged in Tsunami the relief rehabilitation and reconstruction activities on voluntary basis will be accorded following concessions. A Exemption from payment of Value Added Tax on import of goods The Import of goods by Local Donor organizations for Tsunami relief activities will be considered for Tax exemptions on a case by case basis, by the Department of Fiscal Policy. B Deferment of payment of Value Added Tax on Local Purchases using local funds The Value Added Tax deferment system has been extended to Local Donor organizations, though there are no provisions in the VAT Act. Until an amendment is brought in, pending such action, the same scheme introduced for Foreign Funded Donor Agencies (as stated in 1 (B) above will be applicable to such Local Donor organizations as well. In this case, Local Donor organization will be the direct contractor assigned for the work to be carried out. 03. Secretaries to Line Ministries should furnish quarterly progress reports of such recommended projects to the Department of Fiscal Policy. The items of Value Added Tax payment, exempted, differed or paid by the Ministry of Finance & Planning or any other relevant government organization, as per the above instructions, are prohibited to use for any commercial purpose, to be sold, disposed of or transferred to another party without the approval of the Department of Fiscal Policy, Ministry of Finance and Planning.

Higher education and advanced technical education are two vital sectors engaged in the development of advanced human capital in this country. Higher education comprises the University Grants Commission, universities, institutes and post-graduate institutes. Sir, there are 15 universities in the country. There are nine institutes and seven post-graduate institutes engaged in post-graduate education. In other words, a total of 31 higher educational institutions are functioning under the purview of the University Grants Commission, which operates under the purview of the Ministry of Higher Education. In addition, there are two special universities to which I need to advert your attention, the Buddhist and Pali University of Sri Lanka and the Buddhasravaka Bhiksu University in Anuradhapura. I would like to advert my attention to the universities sector which, in my view, is very important and has made a significant contribution to the development process of this country. The initial higher education policy came to be introduced during the colonial period and the traditional colonial university came into existence in two stages. The University College was established in 1921 and the fully-fledged university came into existence or was established in 1942. The university based on the colonial model or the colonial-oriented university was created to cater to the social aspirations of a limited elite in the country and this elite orientation of the university underwent a transformation in the last 50 years; the system has got itself transformed in the last 50 years. The system, though expanded in the '60s and the '70s, in my view, still suffers from certain colonial legacies. There are two sectors: one is the traditional university sector to which I referred - it is the developed sector in the country - and the new universities which came to be built as provincial universities. Initially, the university system emulated the Oxford-Cambridge combination, what we call the Oxbridge combination. We wanted to break away from that Oxbridge model, the Oxbridge mould, and establish universities in the provinces. As a result, provincial universities came into existence. We wanted to break away from the traditional mould with a view to producing an educated workforce with higher levels of skills. This is what we need to do. We need to produce an educated workforce with higher levels of skills, skills which are required in the given market-oriented economic context. Today, Sir, knowledge has become the most important factor in economic development and global socio-political transformation. Knowledge is capital today; knowledge can be marketed today. Therefore, knowledge has become the most important, fundamentally important factor in economic and social development in the world. ( . ) (The Hon. N. Sri Kantha) Mr. Deputy Chairman of Committees, today in the presence of the Hon. Prime Minister and my good Friend, the Hon. Jeyaraj Fernandopulle, a senior Minister of the Government, I would like to make my speech in English so that they would be able to understand our feelings and sentiments to the full measure. Sir, there are twin issues that are being dealt with in the Motion submitted by my good Friend, the Hon. Mavai Senathirajah. One is the abduction of four students while the other is the serious death threat that has been directed at not only the students and certain lecturers of the university, but the Vice-Chancellor as well. This is a very serious situation. I would like to pose only one single question today. What is the answer the Government is going to provide us today in relation to this situation? Four students in their teens have been abducted and we have no doubt whatsoever that certain sections of the armed forces and the running dogs of the armed forces in the Jaffna Peninsula, namely members of the paramilitary groups, are directly, undeniably and indisputably involved in these crimes. The running dogs of the Government, - I am rather compelled to say that and I do not want to hurt the sentiments of the Hon. Ministers, two Gentlemen for whom I have the highest regard - the running dogs of the armed forces there, the Tamil mercenaries who can even go to the extent of pawning their mothers, even to the extent of selling their mothers for their own personal benefits, are directly involved. The Government is answerable. The Government which is in power in the name of the Constitution, by virtue of the Constitution, the legitimate Government, is answerable. In fact, His Excellency the President, I am

thankful, took very serious note when representations were made to him by my Friend, the Hon. Mavai Senathirajah. But, what is the end result? What is it we are having? Is there any information in relation to the abducted youths? Nothing. We want them back and we want them alive! I would like to make it very clear. The Tamil National Alliance demands the release of these four students from wherever they are being detained by the perpetrators of this dastardly crime! We want the university community - the students, the lecturers, the Vice-Chancellor and the staff - to be permitted to proceed with its legitimate business. The university was defunct for a number of months. In fact, we made representations to all concerned including the Vice-Chancellor. I, together with the Hon. Shivajilingam, went to Jaffna a couple of months back in order to ensure that the university would again start functioning. But, what is it that we are having today, Sir? To whom can we complain? Is it to the paramilitary groups, those running dogs in the Jaffna Peninsula? Sir, I would tell the Government, if you cannot solve the problem, please ask your armed forces to step aside. Let them be confined to the barracks for a couple of days and the people of Jaffna would ensure that these boys are released. That is the situation today. This is a very serious situation. We want some remedy, some redress. I think I have the consolation of having the presence of two eminent Ministers including the Prime Minister. Today, as soon as I got up in the morning, when I read in the "Daily Mirror" about the massacre in Delgoda, for a few moments I was filled with sadness. My heart, in fact, went out for the infants who have been hacked to death together with their grandmother, who was 80 years old and the parents. Now, when we feel for these dastardly crimes in the South - I do not know what the motive was - I would expect every Member of the Government to have the same feeling about what has been going on in the North. At Koomankulam in Vavuniya, a couple of days back three youths including a member of my party, a constituent member of the Alliance, namely the TELO, were abducted, tortured for two consecutive days and finally their mutilated bodies with gunshot injuries were displayed for public view at an important junction there. That is the situation in Vavuniya. So, Mr. Deputy Chairman, there is no point in our trying to dodge the issue. We want the Government to tell us straight, are you in a position to control these running dogs; are you in a position to control these mercenaries; are you in a position to control these number one public enemies in Jaffna? I can understand the armed forces fighting the LTTE. That is a different matter altogether. If one is suspected of some LTTE connection, LTTE involvement, the Government can always arrest him or her, lock him up and detain him in
