Having gone into these amendments, I find that despite the fact that we have a provincial set-up and a national set-up in this country, when amendments to

So, what it really means is that in the planning and in the execution of a national project, you do not have support from the provinces. What is important in a development process is the active participation of the people, because if you are building a road through a village or a town, who knows that area better than the people of that area? They would know where the road needs to be strengthened, where the culvert has to be built and what the natural gradient is. Despite having all the professionals coming in, what is important is that the people feel that they are a part of the project and they own it. Now this is the biggest problem in this country. I am not accusing this Government alone. Every government feels that they are the sole authorities for execution of development and they forget that the people who vote them into power have a role to play in developing the land they call their own. Therefore, in these amendments, though members are being increased, the salient feature of bringing in provincial representatives into the national bodies is missing and I am sure the Hon. Minister will take note of that. In fact a Member of the Opposition speaking on this Bill also rightly pointed out that an important sector which also is involved in digging up the roads, the Department of Telecommunications, is not represented in this body. Therefore, I feel those omissions must be made right and you must also ensure that there is participation of the provincial administration in the national bodies. Then again, in reality, we always speak of the Minister in charge nominating an officer. In the case of a Ministry, when a senior officer in the Ministry is chosen, I feel that the executive power of that Ministry, that is the Secretary of that particular Ministry, should be given the responsibility of nominating that officer to be approved by the Minister, because we know that all is not well in all Ministries. Sometimes, the Minister works independent of the Secretary. So, you will have a situation where the Minister will bypass the recommendations of the Secretary and appoint a man who is his favourite. Therefore, I feel that the Secretary of the Ministry should be empowered to nominate the officer and thereafter that can be approved by the Minister. Now, I would like to say something with regard to the operations of the RDA. While going through the website of the RDA, I read their vision statement, mission statement and goals and objectives. All these mean nothing if peoples participation is not brought into the process. My understanding is that all our Government departments work in compartments. Once a Government official is entrusted with a responsibility, there is very little participation from the community which is to be benefited by that exercise. I am not sure how many times

the RDA has had public discussions with regard to the projects that they are planning or implementing, because if you do not educate the people and get the peoples participation right from your planning stage, you cannot execute a successful project. We have seen that. There are many examples for that. I can keep on talking about it. But it does not serve the purpose. If we do not know the lifespan of a road that is being built, if we do not know whether a road is built to last for five years, ten years, twenty years, forty years or hundred years, the way we look at it will change. For example, you take the Mahiyanganaya-Padiyatalawa road. From my childhood I have been driving on that road. There is a patch on this road of about 2 to 3 km which needs yearly maintenance. That road just breaks up and collapses. For the last thirty years or forty years, this has been the trend. No permanent arrangement has been made to arrest that situation. All what we do is that we give out a contract, then the contractor does his job and the road looks nice till it rains again. The fundamental issue there is, that there is seepage under that road. On that stretch of road you have a heavy seepage in the rainy seasons and also there are undercurrents. But that problem is not arrested. Only the surface is tarred all over again. So, obviously the contractors are enriched and of course, every year the people start blaming the Government or the Minister in charge saying that he does not see what is going on. This factor is very important. Another crucial element affecting the condition of roads today is the axel load factor. We are working on a huge plan of paving highways. Now the work of the Oluvil Port project is in progress in the Ampara District. Metal is being transported for that project from as far as Mahiyanganaya. You take it from me, when the next rainy season starts, the stretch of road from Mahiyanganaya to Oluvil will be a mess because nobody is controlling the axel load factor. Nobody is doing that. Nobody knows whether these culverts can hold or not. Nobody is policing that and whoever is trying to stop it will have his palm oiled and the trucks go. The trucks are plying at any time of the day with any payload and every time a speeding vehicle with a heavy load comes and applies its brakes at humps that are erected on this road, it makes a huge impact on the road around that area and after sometime the road will break up. So you have to control the axle load factor and also remove these humps. Now, the East has been freed for almost one and a half years. We do not need these barriers within the Eastern Province. These elements are very important if we are to have roads that can last our lifetime. It is very true that this Government has undertaken a massive project of expanding and modernizing these roads, but modernizing does not end with just carpeting. Modernizing should mean that the old method of working must change. People must own these projects.
