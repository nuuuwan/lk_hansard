Mangala Samaraweera - Minister of Foreign Affairsand Minister of Ports and Aviation)Thank you Mr. Speaker for allowing me to make thisstatement on a matter of great national importance. As youknow Mr. Speaker since the breakout of hostilities in Lebanonlast week, the Ministry of Foreign Afairs, together with ourEmbassy in Lebanon has taken a wide range of measures toprovide emergency assistance to Sri Lankans in areas affectedby the ongoing attacks in that country. There areapproximately, according to reports, 80,000 Sri Lankanscurrently in Lebanon, and the majority are unskilled workers.

The Sri Lankan Embassy in Beirut, since the hostilitiesbroke out a week ago, has set up a 24 hour information deskwhere Sri Lankans can call and seek assistance. Similartelephone inquiry facilities have been set up at the ConsularAffairs Division of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs in Colombowhere Sri Lankans concerned about their family members orrelatives in Bebanon can call for information. I understandthat the SLBFE, that is the Sri Lanka Bureau of ForeignEmployment, has also set up similar facilities. All thesenumbers have already been given publicity through the mediahere in Sri Lanka.

As of Wednesday, Mr. Speaker, approximately 200 SriLankans have sought shelter at the Embassy. They are beingaccommodated and provided with the essential facilitiesincluding food, basis healthcare and local transport at centresrun with the assistance of CARITAS.

In addition, the Embassy has already held discussions withthe local authorities in Beirut and humanitarian agencies suchas CARITAS and IOM with regard to additional safe sheltersin places of religious worship, that could be used, should thenumbers seeking shelter increase. We have also haddiscussions to repatriate Sri Lankans who wish to leave Beirutand let me take this opportunity to thank IOM and CARITASfor their support and co-operation.

The Embassy in Beirut has also been strengthened withadditional local staff; two SLBFE officials and one ForeignMinistry officer to handle inquiries and help Sri Lankansseeking assistance.

The Ministry is working in close co-ordination with theMinistry of Labour, the SLBFE and our Embassies in theregion. Additional funds of Rs. 2.5 million have been allocatedfrom the SLBFE for food, temporary shelter, health, localtransportet cetera for the affected Sri Lankans.

I regret to inform that one confirmed death has beenreported so far of a Sri Lankan national. Details of the next ofkin are awaited to make further arrangements. Our Embassyis working with the Sri Lankan community to ascertain furtherdetails regarding others who may be vulnerable in affectedlocalities.

Steps are also being taken Mr. Speaker, to repatriate SriLankans who have expressed a wish to leave the countryacross the border of Damascus, Syria by bus. Many of themhave arrived in Damascus already and this morning a SriLankan Airlines plane will leave for Damascus to airlift thesepeople back to Colombo.

In fact, we have made arrangements to dispatch a SriLankan Airlines aircraft and during the course of the day, Ibelieve at 11.30 a.m. this morning an A330 Airbus will fly toDamascus and bring back the first batch of Sri Lankansnumbering about 300.

Let me also take this opportunity to thank the Chairmanand the CEO of Sri Lankan Airlines for extending theirassistance to our countrymen in Beirut in their hour of need.Foreign governments concerned have also been contactedwith regard to assisting a possible large-scale evacuation ofSri Lankans out of Lebanon, particularly with regard to safepassage and provision of ships should the need arise.

In fact my Ministry has been in touch with the Governmentof India, the Government of United States of America, theGovernment of Italy and the Government of Turkey on thepossibility of naval evacuations of Sri Lankans from Beirut ifthe need arises. In fact the Government of Israel has alsoassured us of safe passage for vessels carrying Sri Lankansdespite the naval blockade.

The Government of Sri Lanka has expressed the hope thatthe parties involved in hostilities would ease the situationand work towards a cessation of attacks and counter attacks.We have also expressed support to the United Nations andothe regional efforts for bringing about a cessation of violenceand the resolution of issues at hand.

Thank you.
