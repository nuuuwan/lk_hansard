Fowzie - Minister of Petroleum and Petroleum Resources Development) Venerable Sir, I am thankful that I am given this opportunity to speak a few words of a Friend whom I knew for a long time, the late Parliamentarian, the Hon. Sripathi Sooriyaarachchi, who had an untimely and unfortunate death shocking the entire country. He was a skilled debater and a vociferous politician who spoke out his mind freely and fearlessly as mentioned by the previous speakers. He rendered a great service to his electorate, looking after the interests of the people who supported him and did not support him. He won the confidence of those people and became a Member of Parliament. Leave alone his politics, he was respected by all the people. With all his criticism against the Government, even His Excellency the President and the Hon. Prime Minister expressed their sorrow and condolences at his death. He was easily accessible at any time. If anybody wanted to see him, they could meet him and discuss their problems. He was also very popular because of his friendly attitude which helped him to win over people of all communities and be close to him. He was primarily concerned of the welfare of the people of his electorate and all over the country irrespective of caste, creed, race or political affiliation. Men of his calibre are very few and very rare. He was a peoples man who untiringly toiled for the welfare of his people. It is unfortunate that Sripathis promising future of a political career had been shattered by rash decisions. He took a decision which nobody appreciated but he thought that it was the correct decision and his life too ended in a crash with rushing speed.

Venerable Sir, I join the Members of this House in paying my tribute to him and convey my deepest condolences on his demise to the members of his family. Thank you. ( ) (The Presiding Member) Thank you, Hon. Minister. The Hon. John Amaratunga, please.

[.. 11.30] ( ) (The Hon. John Amaratunga) Venerable Sir, the House today we are taking up the Vote of Condolence of the late Member of Parliament and a former Minister, the Hon. Sripathi Sooriyaarachchi.

He, as I knew was a very capable, efficient and shrewd politician. I leave it at that for the moment. He was born and bred at Kandana, a village where I was also born and bred. He was an old boy of St. Anthonys College, Wattala, who had a sound education and a Catholic as well as a Christian background. Subsequently, he ended up as a lawyer and latterly he served as the Commissioner-General of Essential Services. It was at that time I came to know the whereabouts of the late Hon. Sripathi Sooriyaarachchi. From there onwards he climbed the ladder. He contested the Gampaha District and succeeded. He was appointed as a Member of Parliament and thereafter as a Minister. To my knowledge, he rendered a wonderful service as a Member of Parliament for the Kelaniya Electorate. Much of the needs of the Kelaniya Electorate were met by him. In that short period that he was a Member of Parliament, in the broader sense the Gampaha District, confined to the Kelaniya Electorate. If you ask the people of Kelaniya today, they still regret that they have lost a good politician, a hardworking Member of Parliament and a man who was committed to the peoples needs. There is no debate on that; he did a lot.Though he may have been on the opposite side of what I am - he was in the Government and I was in the Opposition - we appreciated that. There is no reason why we should look down upon whatever good things he has done because he is in a different camp. Very unfortunately, he could not complete the numerous projects that he initiated. They were left half done at the time he lost his life. Therefore, all in all, we see that the country has lost a good politician; the country has lost a worker, a person who was committed to work for the welfare and the betterment of the people. We are very sorry about that.

In early 2007, he found that he could not stay any longer in the ranks of the UPFA Government because the Government was inconsistent with his line of thinking
