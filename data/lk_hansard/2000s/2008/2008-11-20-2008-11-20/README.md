Mr. Speaker, I would like to bring to your kind attention the following matter under the Parliament (Powers and Privileges) Act. I happened to drive my car up to the main entrance of the Parliamentary Complex. I inquired from a police officer at the entrance of the whereabouts of my security team and the driver. I was informed that he did not see them. I was trying to move my car further to the public car park when a police officer whistled to stop my movement. I got out from my car and introducing myself as a Member of Parliament and the Deputy Minister of Livestock Development explained that I was looking for my security team and driver. I sought his help to find out my team. The police officer rudely informed me that it was not his part of responsibility and ordered me not to move further. I requested the police officer to remove the barrel that was blocking my forward movement. At that time another police officer appeared and ordered me to remove my car from there immediately. Due to this ill-treatment meted out to me by the police officer, I was forced to wait there all alone without any security for more than 20 minutes. I presume that security arrangements within the Parliament and outside the Parliament area are to protect the parliamentarians and, at times we, Parliamentarians are allowed to proceed through the security zones. However, it seems that we are not protected within the Parliament compound. The police officers had faild to provide me protection. Accordingly, I wish to inform this House that the said action on the part of the police officer tantamounts to a breach of my Parliament Privileges. I therefore, would like to request the House that disciplinary action be taken against the police officers for failing to give me protection. I also would like to complain that the media, mainly, The Island and the Divaina newspapers, have been biased on this episode. Thank you.
