. ( . ) (The Hon. A. Vinayagamoorthy) Hon. Deputy Chairman of Committees, thank you very much for giving me an opportunity to participate in the Debate regarding the extension of Emergency. As usual, we, the TNA Members, oppose this extension and will vote against this extension.

The Hon. Prime Minister has not given sufficient or plausible reasons for extending the Emergency. Emergency in a country can be declared only when there is an emergency situation prevailing in the country. As no emergency situation exists in Sri Lanka now, the Government does not need the Emergency law to govern the country. Except for a short time when the late Premadasa was the President, the country was being ruled under the Emergency law for a very long period.

The Hon. Minister, Susil Premajayantha seems to have asked the UNP whether they will accept the European Union Terms for getting the GSP Plus facility. The extension of Emergency is one of the reasons stated by the EU Terms for not granting the GSP Plus facility.

The European Union had laid down fifteen conditions for granting GSP Plus facility. They are as follows:

1. Reduce the number of derogations to the ICCPR;

2. Implement the Seventeenth Amendment;

3. Repeal the Emergency Regulations;

4. Repeal the Prevention of Terrorism Act;

5. Repeal Clauses 8 and 9 of the Public Security Ordinance;

6. Amend the Code of Criminal Procedure;

7. Allow individuals to submit complaints to the UNHRC;

8. Implement the outstanding opinions of the UNHRC;

9. Extend invitation to the UN Special procedure;

10. Respond to individual cases pending before the UN on

enforced disappearances;

11. Publish final report on the 2008 Commission of Inquiry;

12. Publish names of former LTTE combatants in detention;

13. Provide access to all places of detention for monitoring

purposes;

14. Adopt the National Human Rights Action Plan; 15.Bring about media freedom.

These conditions are very reasonable and these are important demands, and whether the SLFP is ruling, the UNP is ruling or the JVP is ruling, these conditions are very essential to govern the country properly.

It is when the Emergency was in force that the Member for the Batticaloa Municipal Council, Mr. Pragasam Sahayamany seemed to have been abducted in Batticaloa and the TMVP is complaining about this abduction.

An armed group of people snatched the passports of a family who had come to Jaffna after living in London for 15 years and they demanded Rs. 50 lakhs as ransom to release the passports. The family consists of five members and all the passports had been snatched by the people who came armed. What has the Police and the Army done in this matter? If such acts are committed in Jaffna by an armed group of people openly, what is the use of keeping the Police or the Army there?

I also want to state in this connection, that nearly 492 Tamils have gone to Canada as refugees in a ship risking their own lives. The reason is that they are unable to live peacefully in this country. When people flee the country like this and go elsewhere for safety, what will the international community think of this Government? This has happened earlier in Vietnam and Indonesia. A ship from Sri Lanka has also gone to Australia sometime back.

The next matter I want to speak is with regard to the release of prisoners. According to KP, who seems to be an adviser to the Defence Secretary, there are only 1,300 LTTE persons. If that be so, why cannot the rest of the persons who are in remand be released at least on bail with stringent conditions? There are some cases, though important, which have dragged for nearly 10 or 12 years. At least, these people can also be bailed out on stringent conditions and if this is done, we can remove the congestion in the prisons and reduce the number of prisoners. If KP and Karuna could be released, I cannot

Referring to the internal war which ended at Mullivaikkal in May 2009, the outgoing UN Under-Secretary-General for Humanitarian Affairs and Emergency Relief Coordinator, John Holmes, in a farewell press briefing has said that a lot of questions remain unanswered including matters relating to civilian casualties.
