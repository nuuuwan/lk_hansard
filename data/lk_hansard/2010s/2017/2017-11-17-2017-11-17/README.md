( ( - ) The Hon. Vasantha Senanayake - State Minister of Foreign Affairs) Sir, the Stafford Sri Lanka School in Doha is being managed by a group of Sri Lankan community members under a Board of Directors. The school, as per Qatari regulations is considered as a Community School and its financial and administrative transactions should come under the supervision of the Embassy.

The Sri Lanka Embassy and the Stafford Sri Lanka School in Doha had a dispute over the management and supervisory role of the Embassy for some time. During the recent State visit to Qatar, His Excellency the President, Health Minister, the Hon. (Dr.) Rajitha Senaratne, other Ministers and myself visited the Stafford School in Doha on 26th October, 2017 and the following decisions were taken:

(01) The Ambassador to withdraw all charges against

Mr. Kumudu Fonseka.

(02) Mr. Fonseka to submit reports regularly to the

Embassy on administrative and financial matters related to the school.

(03) Mr. Fonseka to include an official from the

Embassy in his Board of Governors.

Following this settlement, Mr. Kumudu Fonseka has been informed by the Ambassador to make a request from him to withdraw the case based on the settlement referred to above.

I was informed by my State Secretary, Mrs. Grace Asirwatham that Mr. Kumudu Fonseka has already informed her that the Ambassador has agreed to withdraw the said charges levelled against him.

In the light of the above, it is clear that the matter between the Sri Lanka Embassy and the Stafford Sri Lanka School in Doha has been resolved amicably. I have also advised the Ministry officials to follow up on this matter.

I reiterate that it is the intention and firm commitment of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs to streamline the smooth functioning of this school, and to look after the welfare of the Sri Lankan community and their families abroad.
