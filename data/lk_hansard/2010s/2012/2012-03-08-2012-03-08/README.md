Our Party welcomes, as the preceding Members who spoke before me stated, the Demerit Points System that you have introduced. However, I wish to speak about the Regulations that you have brought up to set up a Reward and Incentive Fund. Presently, whatever fines that are paid are paid to courts and I believe they become court fines which are paid to

However, with the introduction of this Reward and Incentive Fund, I believe the monies would be accruing to the Department of Motor Traffic, who would share it with the staff in the Department under one category up to 45 per cent and under another category up to 95 per cent. But, it is not clear how this Fund is going to be raised. It is important that because funds are coming into a department, the amount that is coming to this Fund should be made clear to all. These are important issues.

The Hon. Minister also said that he is introducing modern air-conditioned slow buses as luxury buses to Colombo. Ours is one country in this region or one country in the world maybe, where even in the Capital City, we do not have a rapid transport system. We have a railway system that is catering to office-goers to some extent but not many are using it mainly due to the slow speed of our trains.

Under these circumstances, the Minister of Transport should look into the possibility of introducing rapid transport systems. In the City of Colombo, nearly 60 per cent of the vehicles that enter Colombo have less than two passengers. This is causing heavy traffic condition in the city and with more and more vehicles being imported and more and more vehicles coming into the city it is going to be even more difficult in the future. While commending you for bringing in this piece of legislation and attending to three issues that are important for your Transport Ministry in one gazette notification, I would like a clarification as to whether you have stepped into taking away the court funds from Provincial Councils to the Central Government under the Ministry of Transport.
