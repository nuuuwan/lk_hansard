Hon. Deputy Speaker, contract labour has been rapidly spreading at a mass scale threatening job security in Sri Lanka. It is estimated that only 25 per cent of the workforce including public sector employees are in regular employment and this has been brought to the notice of the National Labour Advisory Council by a number of trade unions, and most trade unions are of the view that all workers, whether they are permanent, temporary or casual, shall be directly employed and absolute responsibility for the payment of wages and all statutory and other dues of such workers shall rest with the principal employer. There have been recommendations about this by the ILO itself. The National Labour Advisory Council had highlighted in 2006 the need to frame a legislation to prevent workers under contract labour being deprived of their right dues provided and recognized by our laws and practices.

I now refer to Recommendation No. 198 of the International Labour Organization where it is stipulated that all national laws and practices should be clear and adequate to ensure effective protection of workers in an employment relationship, taking into account relevant international standards.
