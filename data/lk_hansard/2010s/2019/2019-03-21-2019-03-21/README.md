As such, the Appropriation Bill for 2019 recognized the fact that the Samurdhi, which was started in 1994 to facilitate the poor to graduate to a higher income level, has failed not only to protect the vulnerable groups, but also to strengthen and support them. It has now become politicized to such an extent that many Samurdhi officials are strongly affiliated to a certain political party. However, owing to natural disasters and other adverse weather conditions, there were considerable shortfalls in paddy production, compelling the Government to allow import of rice to equalize the consumption requirement. As such, farmers and producers have been faced with severe financial difficulties, becoming heavily indebted or losing their livelihood altogether.

Prime Minister and executed by the Hon. (Dr.) Harsha De Silva, the Government will allocate Rs. 1 billion to implement a concessionary programme to address the severe financial crisis faced by small and medium-scale rice millers and to give a solution to the problem of non-competitive stocks of paddy in the market. I would like to extend my sincere gratitude to the Hon. Prime Minister and the Hon. (Dr.) Harsha De Silva for ensuring an effective mechanism devised to address this burning issue, thereby ensuring that those small to medium-scale rice producers are given their fair share, all the while creating the need for trade competitiveness and growth.
