Speaker, a special Sitting of Parliament has been called today in order to discuss and debate the Proclamation relating to the Emergency Regulations which have been renewed every month since the unfortunate terrorist attacks that took place on 21st of April, 2019.

In the context of the large number of people who were killed as well as injured on that day, declaration of Emergency was not only an inevitability, but also an absolute necessity. It was the additional powers given to the police and the Army through the Emergency Regulations that enabled the security forces to expeditiously dismantle the terrorist network and take into custody those who were involved with the terrorists. However, the Emergency must be renewed and kept in force only as long as it is necessary for the purpose of national security. Once the need is fulfilled, it must be lifted immediately. The President will obviously be guided by the advice tendered to him by the security forces with regard to the need to continue the Emergency. Prolonged use of the Emergency will only lead to abuse of the powers conferred on the security forces and therefore, must be monitored closely.

During the post-21st of April, 2019 period, there have been several persons who have been taken in for questioning. Many of them were not involved with any of those who planned the attacks and therefore, are innocent. We, therefore, urge the Government to speed up the process of investigation and either indict the suspects or in the alternative, release them.

Another important matter that has to be dealt with is the issue of compensation in respect of those who died or were injured as a result of the Easter Sunday attacks. I strongly urge the Government to ensure that compensation is paid expeditiously to those who were victims of the terrorist attacks. Compensation is not sufficient to restore their lives to what it was before the attacks, but, at least, it will mitigate their suffering.

The Government must also look into the damage caused as a result of the attacks on businesses, places of worship and houses of Muslims in Minuwangoda and other areas of the Kurunegala District by organized

groups of thugs on 13th and 14th of May. If the process cannot be speeded up through the usual channels, it may be advisable to organize a mobile service of the relevant government departments to visit the area in order to speed up matters.

I also wish to bring to the notice of the House that despite the existence of the Emergency, a campaign of hate against the Muslims is going on without respite. We hear of Muslims being harassed when they go to transact business in government departments or other public institutions despite circulars and other directions given by the Government with regard to the dress code to be followed by them. Various politicians and religious personalities are not averse to using Islamophobia to advance their electoral and other causes. Hence, as a result, the Muslim community is feeling very insecure. So, the Government owes it to the country to ensure that the security of all the citizens, including the Muslims is ensured with or without the Emergency. Thank you, Sir.
