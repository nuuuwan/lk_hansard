It prevails in Jaffna; it prevails in Mannar; it prevails in Vavuniya; it prevails in Mullaitivu; it prevails in Trincomalee; it prevails in Batticaloa and even in the Tamil parts of Ampara. But, the worst is Jaffna. In the Jaffna Electoral District, several tens of thousands of people have been displaced and are living in different parts of the country on account of the war and extraordinary circumstances. They were the victims of that war. It is something which they had to face willy-nilly. A large number of people have fled the country, they live outside the country, they are not registered as voters and consequently, there has been a substantial depletion in the voting strength of the people in the Jaffna Electoral District.

Now, this has resulted in the number of Members to be elected to the Jaffna Electoral District under the present system decreasing not by one, but probably by two. Definitely, it will be two and I hope it will not be more than two, but at least two. This event is occurring as a result of a circumstance completely beyond the control of these people, in an extraordinary situation where those people had to face the war. Therefore, it is our contention that insofar as the forthcoming election is concerned and perhaps a few more forthcoming elections, there must be arrangements made that if an electoral reform law is being enacted, it will enable the depletion in the voting strength in the Jaffna Electoral District, which has occurred as a result of the war, not to have an adverse impact on the legitimate representation of those people in Parliament, in the supreme Legislature, as at least prevailed on the last occasion. On the last occasion, we had nine seats. According to the electorates, we would have had 11 seats. But, it is my submission that insofar as the forthcoming election is concerned, if there is an electoral reform law going to be enacted, in that law there must be a specific provision which enables the retention of the number of Members to be returned from the Jaffna Electoral District at least at nine, which was the number of Members elected in the year 2010. I think that is a very fair request to make.

In fact, when this matter had been discussed at the Leaders of political parties and when I urged this argument, it had been accepted by the Hon. Prime Minister, the Hon. Ranil Wickremasinghe; it had been accepted by the Hon. Dinesh Gunawardena, who was the Chairman of the Select Committee on Electoral Reform; it had been accepted by the Hon. (Ven.) Athuraliye Rathana Thero and several other leaders including the Left leaders.

Therefore, Sir, I think there is a fair consensus on this matter, that the people of the Jaffna Electoral District must not be penalized for a situation completely beyond their control, where a large number of them are not able to be voters now either because they are dead or because they are not in the country or in their district; they have

been displaced. But, they still remain citizens of Sri Lanka and, therefore, that number should remain, at least, at nine until such time - maybe, for a period of 15 or 20 years - that these people are able to come back and exercise their franchise in these areas as they have done in the past.

Sir, the other question of some concern, which is a matter that has been raised by some of the minority parties, is the question of a double vote: one vote in the polling division, where you choose a particular person as your Member of Parliament and also a vote for a party to enable you to give your democratic expression in respect of the party which you support. You might support an individual, who is a candidate in a particular polling division, for several reasons. He maybe an outstanding person; he maybe an influential person; he maybe a rich person and you may, therefore, support him. There, you make a choice of an individual to be returned, to be elected as your Member for a particular polling division. But, that should not disentitle a person to also express his preference in respect of the party that he supports by having another vote where he can vote for the party. As a matter of fact, it would appear under the proposed law, there will be one nomination paper, but the nomination paper will be in two parts. In one part, there will be the names of the candidates who appear for the different polling divisions, and in the other part, there will be the names of the candidates who are going to be elected on the basis of Proportional Representation increased by three. So, even the nomination paper is divided into two parts: one in respect of those who are contesting in the respective polling divisions, and the other containing the names of persons who could be elected under the Proportional Representation system. So, even when the nomination paper contemplates the candidates in these two categories, I would think that the request for two ballot papers to ensure that a voter is also able to express his preference for the party that he supports through the ballot is important and fundamental, and must be recognized.

Sir, having made these submissions, it would be my view that if we get through with this Twentieth Amendment, the points I have made will be considered when the Amendment is finally presented to Parliament, or will be considered in Parliament at the appropriate stage and will be accommodated.

Thank you very much, Sir.
